Module 4 - capturing and evaluating impact

Each visitor was encouraged to answer three questions at the shop entrance, and the same questions again at the pop-up shop exit. This allowed us to determine our visitors' pre-existing knowledge about antibiotics, antimicrobial resistance and how vaccines work - and whether they changed their answers based on their experience in the shop. Preliminary analysis shows that visitors improved their knowledge by 15-20% on average. A giant model of a bacterium at the exit invited visitors to leave post-it notes with comments and suggestions.

Prof Matthias Eberl
Division of Infection & Immunity

I think the most important lesson I learnt from working with my PPI partners was the importance of taking time, before the study started, for us all to get to know each other. This was over the course of two online meetings to discuss their lived experience and their experience of public involvement.
