Welcome

Our new seven module PPIE Training Programme and Resource Hub will help you to build confidence, skills, and practical tools to embed meaningful, ethical, inclusive and impactful PPIE in your research and education: 

  • Training modules and resources tailored for your CBLS roles
  • Content for all career pathways (researchers, educators, professional service staff) and post graduate research students

Importance of PPIE in the College of Biomedical Life Sciences

PPIE underpins our research and teaching activities in the College of Biomedical and Life Sciences (CBLS).

The College of Biomedical and Life Sciences aims to be a world-leading centre of academic and clinical excellence, renowned for holistic, student-focused education and speciality training targeted at improving the health and well-being of society.

We also strive to be world-leaders in meaningful, ethical, inclusive and impactful Patient and Public Involvement and Engagement (PPIE).

The College is committed to increasing public understanding of and involvement in the biomedical and life sciences, and developing relationships with people, local communities, and organisations. By involving and engaging the public in our research and education, we can be sure that our work meets the needs of our population and delivers meaningful benefits.

Why have we Developed the PPIE Training Programme?

A wide range of high-quality PPIE resources exist, but it can be difficult and time consuming to find the right information when you need it.

We also appreciate the unique needs of the College of Biomedical Life Sciences, as we often involve patients as well as the general public. Our key funders and regulators have specific definitions of involvement and engagement, and it is important that we develop training that reflects this.

A tailored resource was also identified as a top ten PPIE priority for 2025/26 in our School of Medicine PPIE needs and priorities exercise.

Read: "Identifying School of Medicine PPIE Needs and Priorities"

The CBLS PPIE Training Programme

Bringing together existing resources and new content in one place, providing:

  • Centralised training and resources for all your PPIE needs
  • Standardised and up-to-date information on best practices
  • Improved relevance to your work in the College of Biomedical Life Sciences
  • A comprehensive resource with a richer and more diverse understanding of PPIE
  • Opportunities to identify and engage with colleagues doing similar work
  • Learning opportunities from real world case studies collated from across the College

How was the PPIE Training Programme Developed?

Co-Production

We co-developed the training programme with researchers, educators, digital experts, professional service staff, patients, members of the public and external PPIE partners through a series of co-production workshops between January and September 2025. This was overseen by a core development group.

We pilot tested the training programme with XX staff and XX postgraduate research students in September 2025 and used the feedback to refine the resource.

Each module was co-led by an academic lead(s) and at least one public partner lead. Content contributors included public partners, staff, students, external PPIE partners (e.g. NIHR) and external experts working in patient or public involvement, engagement and communications.

Needs Assessment

Training needs covered by the training programme were identified via:

  • Consultation with Senior Leadership Teams in the School of Medicine (December 2023 - May 2024)
  • Discussion with the School of Medicine PPIE Committee Members (July 2024)
  • Results of PPIE Needs Assessment Survey (completed by 82 School of Medicine staff / postgraduate students in Winter 2024)
  • Consulting with School of Medicine PPIE Network members (85 members in April 2025)
  • Consulting with PPIE leads across the College of Biomedical Life Sciences

Resources

Existing resources for inclusion were identified via people involved in co-producing the training programme, our School of Medicine PPIE Network, searches of known PPIE organisations, targeted literature searches (e.g. resources for lab-based research), PPIE leads across the CBLS and the websites of key funders and regulators.

If you feel that we are missing key resources or would like us to showcase your work, please email: medicengagement@cardiff.ac.uk

Maintenance

We will conduct a formal review of the resource annually. We have embedded a 'report an issue' function for users to report any issues encountered. Monthly, we conduct checks to ensure all links, accessibility features, and documents work.

PPIE Training Programme Authors and Contributors

Authors and Contributors

This comprehensive resource has only been possible due to the incredible support of colleagues, students and public contributors. We also received a UKRI Impact Acceleration Account Award which supported PPI, external input, and our PPIE Showcase (November 2025). 

Staff across the College of Biomedical Life Sciences have also volunteered their time, on top of their existing research and teaching commitments, to contribute - developing content, sharing high quality resources, and sharing their invaluable learning and experiences through 48 informative Learning Case Studies. These have informed the quotes included throughout, top tips, guidance and the Learning Case Study library.

We are incredibly grateful for their willingness to dedicate their time to support our ambition for meaningful, ethical, inclusive and impactful PPIE across the CBLS.
